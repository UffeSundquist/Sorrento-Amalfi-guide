SITA Sud Buses

Loading data status…

The primary road transport network operated by SITA Sud, connecting all coastal towns year-round. Validate your ticket before boarding — inspections are frequent.

ℹ️ Buy tickets at tabaccherie, bars or newspaper stands bearing the SITA Sud logo before boarding. The GoEav app also sells tickets. Buses on the SS163 Amalfi Drive can be very crowded in summer — plan extra time.

Local Buses — Sorrento & Amalfi

Town-level bus networks serving the urban areas of Sorrento and Amalfi, operated by EAV and municipal services. Separate tickets from SITA intercity routes.

ℹ️ Local bus tickets are sold at tabaccherie and newsstands. In Sorrento, the main local bus hub is at Piazza Lauro and the train station. In Amalfi, the central bus stop is in Piazza Flavio Gioia by the harbour.

Ferry Routes from Sorrento

The most scenic way to travel the coast. Ferries depart from Marina Piccola in Sorrento — a 5-minute walk from Piazza Tasso. Services run April–October; Capri and Naples routes operate year-round.

ℹ️ Ferries depart from the central ports of each town. Buy tickets at the pier kiosk before boarding — most routes do not allow on-board purchase. Services may be cancelled in rough seas.

Circumvesuviana from Sorrento

The commuter railway departing from Sorrento station toward Naples, stopping at Pompeii and Herculaneum. All times shown are from Sorrento outbound and back to Sorrento inbound.

ℹ️ Buy tickets at Sorrento station ticket window or kiosks (cash only for standard tickets). All services depart from Sorrento station platform. The Campania Express tourist train offers air-con and reserved seats from mid-March to mid-October — book in advance at eavsrl.it. At Naples Garibaldi on return, always check the board: you want the SORRENTO train, not Sarno or Poggiomarino.

🚌 SITA Bus — Unico Costiera Fares

🛒
Where to buy: Tabaccherie (tobacconists), bars and newsstands displaying the SITA Sud sign — always buy before boarding. App: unicocampania.it and the Unico Campania app (iOS/Android). No refunds on used tickets. Validate immediately on boarding in the yellow machine. Unico Costiera tickets cover SITA buses on the Amalfi Drive only — not Circumvesuviana trains or ferries.

🚐 Local Bus — Sorrento & Amalfi Fares

🛒
Where to buy: Tabaccherie and bars near major bus stops. In Sorrento the main EAV local bus ticket offices are at Piazza Lauro and the train station. App: eavsrl.it and the GoEav app (iOS/Android) for Circumvesuviana and EAV bus tickets. Day passes and return tickets are not generally available for local town buses — single-journey only.

🚆 Circumvesuviana (EAV) Fares

🛒
Where to buy: Ticket machines and counters at all Circumvesuviana stations — Sorrento, Sant'Agnello, Meta, Pompei Scavi, Torre Annunziata, Ercolano, Napoli Garibaldi. App: eavsrl.it and GoEav app. Return tickets are available and represent a small saving — ask for "andata e ritorno". Weekly pass (Abbonamento Settimanale EAV): available from ticket offices for frequent use; worth buying for stays of 5+ days with daily Naples or Pompeii visits. Campania Artecard (see Metro section) also covers Circumvesuviana.

Napoli Metro

Naples has two underground lines useful for tourists (Line 1 and Line 2), plus four funicular railways climbing the city's hills. The same ticket system covers all of them — metro, bus, tram and funicular.

ℹ️ Always validate your ticket before boarding. On buses and trams, use the yellow stamping machines on board. At metro and funicular stations, barriers stamp automatically. Inspectors operate frequently — an unvalidated ticket is treated as no ticket.
🎫 Full ticket prices for the Napoli metro, bus and funicular network — including the all-day pass (€4.50) and Campania Artecard (€21) — are listed in the Prices tab under "Napoli Metro, Bus & Funicular Fares".

🛒 Where to Buy

🏪
Tabaccherie & newsstands
The easiest option — look for a blue "T" sign. Over 500 authorised sellers across Naples sell all ticket types in cash. Buy a handful at once to avoid queues later.
🎰
Ticket machines at stations
All metro stations and funicular stops have machines with English-language menus that accept cash and cards. Machines at Garibaldi (the start of Line 1) are often very busy — buy elsewhere if you can.
📲
Tap & Go contactless
Most metro barriers support contactless bank card or phone payment — tap your card or phone directly at the gate. Useful in a pinch, but paper tickets are faster when barriers are busy.
🖥️
ANM app & online
The official ANM app and website sell digital tickets. The Campania Artecard can be pre-purchased online at campaniartecard.it and activated on arrival — strongly recommended.

🚇 The Lines Explained

🟡
Line 1 — The Art Metro
The main tourist line. Runs from Garibaldi (Napoli Centrale) westward through the historic centre, stopping at Università, Municipio, Toledo, Dante and Museo, then climbing north to the Vomero hill. Trains every 10–14 minutes, 06:20–23:00 (until 01:00 on Saturdays). Toledo is considered one of Europe's most beautiful metro stations — don't just pass through it.
🔵
Line 2 — Suburban Rail
Runs from Pozzuoli in the west to Gianturco in the east, passing through Piazza Amedeo (useful for the Chiaia neighbourhood) and connecting to Line 1 at Garibaldi. Requires a 90-minute integrated TIC ticket (€1.80), not the basic single ticket. Less useful for most tourists than Line 1.
🟠
Four Funiculars
Four cable railways climb Naples' steep hills. Centrale and Chiaia connect the centre to Vomero (for Castel Sant'Elmo and San Martino monastery). Montesanto departs near the Museo metro stop. Mergellina links the waterfront to the Posillipo hill. Same tickets as the metro — a single €1.30 ride or use your day pass.
🎨
The Art Stations
Line 1 is known as the Metropolitana dell'Arte — each station was designed by a different contemporary architect or artist. Toledo (by Oscar Tusquets, with its cosmic mosaic shaft) is the most celebrated. Also worth pausing at Università (Karim Rashid), Municipio, Dante and Garibaldi. Admission to the stations is free — you just need a valid ticket.

💡 Using the Metro from Sorrento

From Hotel Girasole (Sant'Agnello), the Sant'Agnello Circumvesuviana stop is a 5-minute walk east along Corso Italia. Take any train toward Naples (~1h, €4.90) to Napoli Garibaldi, which sits directly above Metro Line 1's eastern terminus — walk downstairs to connect. Alternatively, walk 10 minutes to Sorrento station and take the ferry from Marina Piccola to Molo Beverello (~45min, €12–15) — the port is a short walk from Municipio metro station (Line 1), ideal for starting at Toledo and the historic centre.
Day strategy: Buy a €4.50 all-day pass at a tabaccheria near Sant'Agnello or Sorrento station before you leave — it covers the metro, buses and funiculars for the whole day in Naples. Note it does not cover the Circumvesuviana or the ferry; those are separate tickets.

☕ At the Bar — Drinks

In Italy, drinks are ordered at the counter (al banco) — it's faster and cheaper. Say what you want, pay when it arrives, and stand to drink it. Sitting at a table costs more.
